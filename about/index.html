About

Why I do this work

I’m Sara — an environmental communicator and program analyst based in Raleigh, North Carolina (open to relocation). I care deeply about advocating for what can’t speak up for itself: protecting human health, defending ecosystems, and preserving the earth for generations to come.

I’m most energized by work that blends clear communication with strong analysis — developing credible, usable insights and making them understandable for all audiences. Over time, I’ve become especially passionate about clean energy and environmental justice: practical solutions that improve lives, reduce harm, and build durable progress.

My story

From Northern Maryland → Raleigh

I grew up in Northern Maryland, where I first started paying attention to how policy, environment, and health intersect in everyday life. That curiosity eventually brought me to Raleigh — and it’s where I’ve continued building a career at the intersection of systems, communication, and climate.

  • Rooted in place I care about the communities we live in — cleaner air, safer water, and resilient infrastructure that supports people over the long term.
  • Curious by nature I’m passionate about being well-rounded and learning across disciplines — communication, data, and spatial tools that make climate work more actionable.
  • Open to the world I’m open to relocation and genuinely excited to see more of the world — new places, new perspectives, new ways of solving problems.

What I’m looking for

Roles where I can grow while contributing to clean energy and environmental justice initiatives — especially work that is grounded in evidence, centered on people, and built to endure.

Where I’m headed

Long term, I want to contribute to research (or research-adjacent) work that helps develop science, advocate for it responsibly, and make it digestible and useful for all audiences.
