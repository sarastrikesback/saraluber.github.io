About

Rooted in care. Driven by clarity.

I’m Sara — an environmental communicator and program analyst based in Raleigh, North Carolina (open to relocation). I’m drawn to work that protects human health and the environment, advocates for what can’t speak up for itself, and preserves the planet for generations to come.

I care deeply about helping people understand complex climate and clean energy issues — and about making science and policy more accessible, more actionable, and more equitable. My sweet spot is working at the intersection of communication and data: translating research into stories, tools, and decisions that hold up over time.

My path

From Northern Maryland to Raleigh — and beyond

The places I’ve lived and learned shaped what I care about: community, public service, and long-term climate solutions.

Where I grew up

I’m originally from Northern Maryland, where I developed a strong appreciation for community, public spaces, and the everyday systems that shape quality of life.

Undergrad: Political Science

I studied Political Science to better understand how policy affects underserved communities — and how inequities persist even within established democracies. That foundation strengthened my empathy and sharpened my interest in justice-centered work.

Building technical depth

After graduating, I earned two professional certificates through IBM to strengthen my technical toolkit and confirm my interest in advancing my education.

Now: Climate Change & Society (M.S.) + GIS

I’m currently pursuing my M.S. in Climate Change & Society at NC State — an interdisciplinary program — with a professional certification in GIS. I’m focused on spatial analysis that supports climate mitigation and environmental justice.

Why this work

Protecting people and the planet

I’m motivated by climate work that is practical, science-based, and equity-centered — work that improves health outcomes, strengthens community resilience, and helps decision-makers act with confidence.

  • Environmental & public health Clear communication that helps people understand risk, exposure, and what to do next.
  • Clean energy + climate resilience Turning evidence into action through programs, incentives, and durable systems.
  • Justice + accessibility Making research digestible and useful for a wide range of audiences — not just experts.

What I’m working toward

I’m building a well-rounded career in communications and data analytics for clean energy and environmental justice initiatives. Long-term, I’d love to contribute in a research-oriented role that helps develop science, advocate for it, and translate it into work people can understand and use.
