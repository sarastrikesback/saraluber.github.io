Rooted in curiosity, care, and community

My path to environmental communications has always been shaped by people. I grew up watching how access to clean air, reliable data, and clear information can make or break a community’s ability to thrive. That perspective grounds every project I take on.

Today I live in Raleigh, North Carolina, and I’m open to relocating so I can keep supporting environmentally focused teams wherever they need me. Outside of work, you’ll find me sewing, baking, reading, and creating experiences that invite people in.

Personal interests that keep me grounded

Sewing & textile design

Pattern drafting and textile sourcing remind me how supply chains touch every community differently.

  • Designing functional pieces for friends and family
  • Hosting mending nights to keep clothes out of landfills
  • Documenting projects to share techniques with others

Baking for connection

Sharing bread, pies, and cookies is my favorite way to bring people together and fuel difficult conversations with generosity.

  • Experimenting with local, seasonal ingredients
  • Coordinating community bake swaps and fundraisers
  • Translating family recipes into accessible guides

Reading & research

Whether it’s policy analysis or speculative fiction, reading widens my understanding of whose voices are centered—and whose are missing.

  • Environmental justice memoirs and reportage
  • Books on systems thinking and data equity
  • Fiction that imagines resilient futures

Why environmental communications matter to me

Everyone deserves access to accurate, plain-language information about the environmental resources they rely on—from drinking water to public transit. I’m driven by the belief that data, policy, and narrative should work together so future generations inherit systems that care for them.

That’s why I gravitate toward roles where I can gather insights, build transparent processes, and tell stories that keep communities at the center. If your team is ready to create communications that respect people’s time and intelligence, I’d love to help.
